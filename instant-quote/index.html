How to grade power
  • Choose "Yes" if the screen lights up, you can unlock the device, and it charges without shutting off.
  • Pick "No" for phones that stay black, are stuck on the logo, or only wake up while plugged in.
  • If the battery is swollen or the phone randomly powers down, treat it as "No" so we can prep a safe shipping kit.
Working screen examples
  • "Yes" if the display is bright, touch responds everywhere, and there are no lines, ghost images, or ink blotches.
  • "No" when part of the display is dim, unresponsive, cracked under the glass, or shows color bleed.
  • Choose "No" if Face ID/Touch ID is disabled because the screen or top sensors were replaced with aftermarket parts.
Cosmetic clues
  • Flawless: looks store-fresh with zero noticeable wear on glass or frame.
  • Scratched: light scuffs, pocket wear, or faint dings but no cracks or chips.
  • Damaged: cracked glass, deep gouges, bent corners, or missing buttons/parts.

What does "Fully Functional" mean?

For a device to qualify as **"Fully Functional"**, all of the following criteria must be met:

  • **Screen & Display:** The screen must work and fully light up (no dead pixels, discoloration, persistent image burn, or black dots/LCD damage).
  • **Original Parts:** The device must contain **all original manufacturer parts**. Devices with third-party, non-original, or aftermarket replacement parts will be considered "Not Functional."
  • **Key Features:** All exterior buttons (power, volume, mute), front/back camera and flashes, speakers, and microphones must function correctly.
  • **Connectivity & Ports:** Charging port, Cellular, Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and GPS must all work.
  • **Biometrics & Touch:** The touchscreen, facial recognition (Face ID), and fingerprint sensor (Touch ID) must be fully operational.
  • **Battery Health:** The battery must hold a sufficient charge for normal operation.

If your device fails any of these checks, please select "No" to the functionality question for an accurate revised offer.
